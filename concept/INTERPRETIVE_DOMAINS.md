# The Interpretive Domains — where the standard actually lives

*Supersedes the "lead with observable enterprise domains" framing. Status: founder directive,
2026-05-31. Revises the lead in `../proposals/0003_lead_with_observable_domains.md` (keeps its
rejection of felt-domain deprecation; changes which surface leads).*

---

## The correction

An earlier review (Gemini) argued: lead V1 with observable enterprise domains — refusal, compliance,
code — and defer the felt ones. That stance was **inherited, not warranted.** It was licensed by
*adoption-optimization* (make enterprise legal comfortable), not by what this standard is *for*. The
standard's purpose is the **interpretive domains**: writing, editing, coaching, teaching, criticism —
the humanities. Under the standard's own engine, a stance carried from the wrong condition is
overhang. So the lead updates.

## Why the interpretive domains are the home, not the hard mode

**The gate is most meaningful exactly where ground truth is hardest.** In a compliance or code
domain, a determinate rule usually exists — an SOP or AOP could check it anyway, which makes the xOP
nearly redundant there. In writing, coaching, teaching, criticism there is *no rule to check*, and
this is precisely where current AI fails: it flatters, it resolves, it flattens a warranted choice
toward generic-correct because that feels like help. The place with no free ground truth is the
place the gate is the only thing standing. xOP is an **interpretive instrument**, not an enterprise
governance primitive.

## The price, stated honestly

Leading with the interpretive domains makes the **observability gap the lead problem, not a deferred
one** (`03_REVIEWER_GUIDE.md`, soft-spot 1). We do not get to lead with coaching and skip the judge.
So we do not lead with coaching. We lead with **writing** — the bridge.

## The ladder of observability inside the interpretive home

| Domain | Is `x` observable? | The license call | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Writing / editing** | **yes** — prose read cold by a stranger | is this choice warranted by the piece, or inherited from a style guide / past register? | **proving ground (flagship)** |
| Teaching / criticism | partly — the explanation/argument is an artifact | is this correction warranted by what the learner needs? | second |
| **Coaching** | **no** — only self-report | is this reaction/limit warranted by what's present? | **deepest, last** — anchor-gated, heaviest abstain |

Writing gives you an observable `x` *and* a fully interpretive license call. Build the interpretive
judge where you can actually measure the residual (read the prose cold), validate the gate there,
then carry it inward toward coaching — which stays gated behind a datable present-condition anchor
and scoped to *stated*-license (`COP_coaching.md`, forthcoming).

## The scope limit that survives the circularity attack

In every interpretive domain the xOP tests whether the **stated** license holds against an anchor.
It does **not** claim to recover the **true** driver. Writing: it can read what the prose *does*
versus what it *claims* — observable. Coaching: it can check whether the *stated* condition is
present and datable — but not whether the stated reason is the real one. So an interpretive xOP
surfaces *stated-license* mismatch and **abstains on driver uncertainty**. That narrower claim is the
honest one; the grander "find the true signal" claim is what reopens the circularity Gemini flagged.

## What this changes, concretely

1. **Flagship shifts** to writing as the interpretive proving ground (`../examples/writing_license/`).
   The refusal xOP stays as the agent-surface exemplar — it is still the cleanest *engineering* case.
2. **`0003`'s lead is amended** from "observable enterprise" to "interpretive, via the writing
   surface." Its rejection of felt-domain *deprecation* stands. Recorded as insight 0002.
3. **The judge's first validation target** is interpretive-but-observable (writing), not refusal.
   This is harder and more honest, and it is the actual point of the project.
