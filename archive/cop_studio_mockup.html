The Inherited-Stance Check
A reflective move for when a reaction outlives the thing that caused it. Surfaces the gap between what's being felt and what's actually in the room — then stops. It does not resolve.
borrowed from agent procedures (AOP) Lyra-native (the defensible layer)
Intent — run only when all holdAOP pattern
AND the person is reacting strongly to a situation, decision, or person
AND the intensity seems out of proportion to what's currently in front of them
AND there is prior history in play (an old role, a past manager, a previous version of this conflict)
Guardrail — do NOT run; hand off insteadAOP pattern → safety use
STOP if the person is in acute distress or crisis → route to a human
STOP if the material is clinical (grief, trauma, mental health) → refer to a licensed professional, reason "clinical_referral"
STOP if the person has explicitly asked for a decision, not reflection
Reflective procedure — hold the pause, one move at a timeLyra-native
1Reflect the reaction back in the person's own words. Do not interpret. hold
2Ask once: "Is this reaction licensed by what's in front of you now — or inherited from somewhere earlier?" Then wait. Do not answer it for them. do not resolve
3If a residual surfaces, name the gap a single time and stop. No problem-solving, no action plan. one pass
Residual targetLyra-native
L = x − x̂behavior − self-description = the gap to surface

Surface where the stated reason ("they're incompetent") and the actual trigger ("they remind me of someone") diverge. The divergence is the residual — name it, don't explain it away.

License checkLyra-native
warranted → trigger still present → respect it, end inherited → trigger gone → surface as overhang undecidable → abstain
Abstain conditions — gatedLyra-native

If you cannot tell warranted from inherited without more data, say so and ask for the data. Never guess toward "you're overreacting."

GATE · false-positive on a warranted reaction must stay 0 — never soften a feeling that's actually licensed just to move the session forward.
Ground-truth binding — so the coach isn't grading the person's own mirrorLyra-native
