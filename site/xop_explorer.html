The standard · xOP

Give your AI a judgment
it won't override.

Pick a variant. Watch it run on a real transcript across the three states it can be in — warranted, inherited, undecidable. It holds, surfaces, or abstains. It never resolves for the sake of resolving.

Why · for the business

An AI that always answers is a liability.

It reassures you past a real risk, agrees with a flawed plan, drops a refusal it should have kept, and tells a stressed leader what they want to hear. Those are the defaults, not edge cases. An xOP makes the AI hold the line on what's actually true — the real limit, the warranted refusal, the accurate read. One rule: it never overrides something that's still warranted. That's the line between an assistant that's agreeable and one you can trust with a judgment.

Why · for the builder

Resolution drift, made a testable invariant.

Every reflection/agent loop drifts toward closing — warmer, more reassuring — and under any imperfect judge it starts overriding states that were correct. xOP turns that into a number you can fail a build on: false_positive_on_warranted == 0, scored against blind-labeled gold, no model grading model. It's a portable spec you paste into any model, with an eval rail that proves a change didn't break the gate. COP, WOP, refusal — one engine, different triggering conditions.
