The core

Driving force

We don't perform.

Everything we sell is a refusal to perform. So the writing has to model it. No filler openings, no hype, no wrapping a sentence up just to feel finished.

The whole product line exists because AI tells people what they want to hear. If our copy does the same thing — flatters, overclaims, pads — we've disproved ourselves on the homepage.

Gut-check every line against one question:

"Is this true — or is it performing?"

If a sentence is there to sound impressive, sound caring, or sound finished rather than to be true, cut it. That's the same move our products make. The voice is the proof.

Four tenets — each is one of Lyra's own scores

Tenet 01

We're plain.

maps to · no_template

Why? Because plainness is the opposite of performance. Jargon and filler are how copy hides that it isn't saying anything. We use small words and short sentences and we don't dress them up.

"Your AI agreed with you. Again."

Plain might sound like — name the problem in the reader's own words.

"Same job. Less theater."

Five words. No adjectives doing the work that a fact should.

Plain-spoken, small words, short linesBlunt, declarative, one-line verdicts
Tenet 02

We commit.

maps to · one_truth

Why? Because hedging is performance wearing a lab coat. We say the one true thing and stand on it, instead of ten qualified maybes. Where we're honestly unsure, we say that plainly — and stop.

"The judge isn't built yet. We say so on the homepage."

Commitment includes committing to the limit. Honesty reads as confidence.

"It will never talk you out of something that's real."

One promise, unhedged. No "we try to" or "in most cases."

Quietly certain, no hedgingFlat declarative, takes a side
Tenet 03

We make contact.

maps to · landing_first + affect_contact

Why? Because generic copy talks at a category; real copy lands on a person in a specific moment. We write to the reader who just watched their AI flatter them — not to "users." We make contact with the actual feeling, and we never fake warmth.

"For the conversation about you — not a task."

The COP page opens on the person, not the mechanism.

"It won't hype you up, and it won't talk you into quitting."

Specific contact with the real fear, both directions. Not "we support you."

Warm, direct, to one personUnsparing, names the hard thing
Tenet 04

We stop clean.

maps to · clean_exit

Why? Because the closing flourish is the most common performance of all. We end when the thought is complete. No "in conclusion," no recap, no CTA that begs. The last line earns its place or it's cut.

"Receipts, not vibes."

A close that's a fact, not a flourish. Then nothing.

"Run one free in two minutes."

The CTA states the action and the cost. It doesn't sell the click.

Economical, ends on the pointAbrupt, one-line drop-off

Say / never say

We say

  • warrant — "is it still warranted?" (never "license")
  • hold, surface, abstain — the three moves
  • the version that doesn't perform (Lyra)
  • the version that doesn't resolve (xOP)
  • no model grades model · receipts, not vibes
  • plain verbs: run, paste, hold, name, stop

We never say

  • magic · magical · effortless
  • AI-powered · revolutionary · game-changing
  • unlock · supercharge · delight · seamless
  • in today's fast-paced world…
  • we're excited to · we're thrilled to
  • license (collides with the OSS LICENSE file)

Worked: what is Lyra?

Before · performing

Lyra is a revolutionary AI-powered quality layer that supercharges your prompts with cutting-edge behavioral intelligence to unlock delightfully human responses across every model.

After · awaken voice

Lyra is a behavior layer for AI instructions. It finds the spots where your prompt makes the model perform — agreeing too fast, over-explaining, wrapping up for the sake of it — and rewrites them. Same deliverable. Cleaner conduct. With receipts.

Worked: Lyra vs xOP, in one breath each

Lyra

The version that doesn't perform. Rewrites how a model behaves while it does any task — less agreement reflex, less filler, cleaner endings.

xOP

The version that doesn't resolve. A procedure for the calls that shouldn't be rushed to an answer — it holds the judgment instead of closing it. Its variants are COP (coaching) and WOP (writing).
