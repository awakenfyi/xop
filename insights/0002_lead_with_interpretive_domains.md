# Insight 0002 — the lead is the interpretive domains, not enterprise observability

- **From:** founder directive, 2026-05-31 ("it has to be coaching — writing and almost humanities").
- **Observation:** The V1 lead was set (via `proposals/0003`) to observable *enterprise* domains
  (refusal, compliance, code) on an adoption argument. The founder's stated purpose for the standard
  is the **interpretive / humanities** domains — writing, editing, coaching, teaching, criticism. The
  enterprise-lead stance was licensed by adoption-optimization, not by the standard's purpose →
  inherited, not warranted.
- **Became:** the reframe in `concept/INTERPRETIVE_DOMAINS.md`, and an amendment to `0003`'s lead.
- **Why it might NOT have mattered (the counter-case, kept honest):** the adoption argument is real —
  interpretive domains are where the observability gap bites hardest, so leading there makes the
  judge harder to validate and slower to prove. The resolution is not to dodge that cost but to pay
  it through the **writing** surface, where `x` (the prose) is observable *and* the call is
  interpretive. We lead interpretive without leading blind.
- **What did NOT change:** `0003`'s rejection of felt-domain *deprecation* stands; nothing here
  reopens deleting the felt license types. Only the *lead surface* changed.

→ Promoted directly to a doc reframe because it is a founder scope decision, not a gate or
license-logic change. The gate, the values, and the floors are untouched.
