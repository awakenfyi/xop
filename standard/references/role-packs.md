# Role Packs — worked Project OS recommendations

Four examples `/xop interview` produces. Each is **a recommendation**, and every xOP in it starts
at `DESIGNED`. The behavior is never "always preserve everything" — it's *deliberate choice →
preserve · demonstrated failure → surface · can't tell → ask, don't normalize.*

## 1 · Editor building a book outline
**Skills:** developmental editing · narrative architecture · chapter-outline construction · argument/evidence mapping.
**Core xOPs:** Keep the Brief (don't silently change premise/reader/genre/outcome) · Ask Only When It Matters (ask when a missing decision changes the architecture; else a reversible assumption) · Done Means Verified (not complete until every chapter has purpose, progression, relation to the central promise).
**Editor xOPs:** Preserve Author Intent (challenge weak execution, don't replace the project) · Approved Decisions Stay Approved (don't reopen settled structure absent new conflicting evidence) · Evidence Matches Claim (sourced vs hypothesis vs illustration vs assertion).
**Guards:** chapter count · POV consistency · chronology · repeated beats · missing argument steps · unsupported citations · required front/back matter.

## 2 · Marketer building an AEO content strategy
**Skills:** audience/query research · topic architecture · answer-first design · editorial calendar · brief generation.
**Core xOPs:** Claims Need Receipts · Work Within Constraints (product truth, expertise, capacity, audience, commercial goals) · Done Means Verified (not complete because a topic list exists).
**AEO xOPs:** Intent Before Volume (a topic must answer a real question, not just match a keyword) · Evidence Before Answer (what the company can substantiate vs what the web merely repeats) · Distinct Value Before Production (don't ship a page that adds nothing specific/authoritative).
**Guards:** source/citation presence · question coverage · duplicate topic intent · required brief fields · title/metadata limits · unsupported superlatives · fabricated statistics.
*The interview also marks which parts need live research vs the company's internal evidence.*

## 3 · Marketer rebuilding a website / brand
*One Brand & Website Project OS, not dozens of disconnected prompts.*
**Skills:** brand discovery · positioning · information architecture · messaging hierarchy · website copy · stakeholder review.
**Core xOPs:** Keep the Brief · Claims Need Receipts · Permission Before Consequence · Done Means Verified.
**Project xOPs:** Decision Ledger (an approved decision stays active until explicitly reopened) · Voice License (preserve brand voice while reference material supports it; don't drift generic) · Change With Provenance (state what changed, why, and which downstream assets are affected) · Stakeholder Authority (not every comment is an equal instruction; respect defined decision rights).
**Guards:** approved terminology · banned phrases · page inventory · required components · CTA count · character limits · accessibility · brand-token use · missing proof points.
*This is where xOP beats a style guide: it governs **when** the guide applies, **who** can change it, and what happens when evidence or strategy changes.*

## 4 · Executive OS — coaching, Slack, presentations, team comms
**Skills:** reflective coaching · decision framing · Slack drafting · board communication · presentation development · difficult-conversation prep.
**Core xOPs:** Address the Actual Concern (no generic reassurance for the real issue) · Status Matches Reality (don't turn "at risk" into "on track" because it's uncomfortable) · Ask Only When It Matters.
**Exec xOPs:** Diagnose Before Prescribing (reflect while they explore; options when they ask for action) · Candor Without Reflexive Contrarianism (challenge contradictions on the record; don't manufacture disagreement to seem rigorous) · Audience and Channel Fit (board vs leadership vs all-hands vs DM vs Slack — different depth/register) · Decision First (lead with the decision/risk/request when that's what the audience needs) · Boundary and Handoff (don't convert coaching into clinical, legal, HR, or crisis intervention).
**Guards:** Slack length · decision appears early · named owner + next action · no generic praise · no invented consensus · presentation structure · required dates/figures.
